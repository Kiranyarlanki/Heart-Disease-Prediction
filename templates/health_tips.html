Heart-Healthy Diet

Foods to Include
  • Fatty Fish: Salmon, mackerel, sardines (2-3 times/week)
  • Leafy Greens: Spinach, kale, collard greens
  • Whole Grains: Oats, quinoa, brown rice, whole wheat
  • Berries: Blueberries, strawberries, blackberries
  • Nuts & Seeds: Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds
  • Olive Oil: Extra virgin olive oil for cooking
Foods to Limit/Avoid
  • Processed Meats: Bacon, sausages, deli meats
  • Trans Fats: Fried foods, margarine, packaged snacks
  • Excess Sodium: Limit to <2,300mg daily
  • Added Sugars: Sodas, candies, pastries
  • Refined Carbs: White bread, white rice, pastries
  • Excessive Alcohol: Limit to 1-2 drinks/day max

Exercise & Physical Activity

Aerobic Exercise

Goal: 150 minutes/week moderate intensity

  • Brisk walking
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Dancing
  • Gardening
Strength Training

Goal: 2-3 days/week

  • Weight lifting
  • Resistance bands
  • Bodyweight exercises
  • Yoga
  • Pilates
Flexibility

Goal: Daily stretching

  • Stretching
  • Yoga
  • Tai Chi
  • Pilates
  • Cool-down walks

Healthy Lifestyle Habits

Quality Sleep
  • 7-9 hours nightly
  • Consistent sleep schedule
  • Avoid screens before bed
  • Cool, dark room
  • Limit caffeine after 2 PM
Stress Management
  • Meditation/mindfulness
  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Social connections
  • Hobbies and relaxation
  • Professional help if needed
Avoid Harmful Habits
  • No smoking/tobacco
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Avoid recreational drugs
  • Reduce sedentary time
  • Limit processed foods
Maintain Healthy Weight
  • Know your BMI
  • Portion control
  • Balanced nutrition
  • Regular exercise
  • Track progress

Prevention & Risk Reduction

Regular Health Screenings
Test Frequency Target
Blood Pressure Every 2 years <120/80 mmHg
Cholesterol Every 4-6 years <200 mg/dL
Blood Sugar Every 3 years <100 mg/dL
BMI Annually 18.5-24.9
Medication Management
  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Don't skip doses
  • Discuss side effects with doctor
  • Regular medication reviews
  • Keep updated medication list

Self-Monitoring & Tracking

Blood Pressure

Monitor at home if recommended by doctor

  • Same time daily
  • Seated, relaxed
  • Keep a log
Weight & BMI

Track weekly for weight management

  • Weekly weigh-ins
  • Same time of day
  • Track trends
Heart Rate

Monitor during exercise and rest

  • Resting: 60-100 bpm
  • Target during exercise
  • Use fitness trackers

Emergency Warning Signs

Call 911 Immediately if you experience:
Heart Attack Symptoms
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Pain in arms, back, neck, jaw
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Cold sweats
Stroke Symptoms (FAST)
  • Face: Facial drooping
  • Arms: Arm weakness
  • Speech: Speech difficulty
  • Time: Call 911 immediately
